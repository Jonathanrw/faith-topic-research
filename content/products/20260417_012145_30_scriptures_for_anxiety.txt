30 Scriptures for Anxiety
A Scripture Pack to Help You Find Peace in the Moment and Build Lasting Calm

For Christians looking for biblical encouragement and peace.
Promise: A scripture-based resource you can return to whenever anxiety hits.

Introduction
This pack gives 30 Scriptures, each with a short reflection and a simple, practical action you can use right away. Read them in a crisis, memorize one a week, or create a pocket list to carry. The tone is warm and direct—no fluff—so you can get to God’s Word and feel steadier fast.

How to use this pack
- Emergency: Keep the first 6–8 verses handy (phone note or card) for immediate relief.
- Daily: Use the 30-day plan at the end to focus one verse each day.
- Memorize: Pick one verse a week to learn by heart.
- Practice: Read the verse slowly, repeat it aloud, breathe deeply between phrases, and follow the short action step.

Quick grounding ritual (use in 2–5 minutes)
1. Breathe: In 4 counts, hold 2, out 6 (repeat 3 times).
2. Read one verse slowly.
3. Do the action step tied to that verse (pray, write, repeat).
4. If helpful, text one line of the verse to a trusted friend or pray it aloud.

30 Scriptures (Verse — Reflection — Action)
1) Philippians 4:6-7 (KJV)
"Be careful for nothing; but in every thing by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known unto God. And the peace of God, which passeth all understanding, shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus."
Reflection: Pray and thank God—peace follows prayer.
Action: Pray this verse aloud, listing 3 things you’re thankful for.

2) Matthew 6:25 (KJV)
"Therefore I say unto you, Take no thought for your life, what ye shall eat, or what ye shall drink..."
Reflection: Jesus tells us not to carry worry about daily needs.
Action: Write one worry and intentionally hand it to God in prayer.

3) Psalm 94:19 (KJV)
"In the multitude of my thoughts within me thy comforts delight my soul."
Reflection: God’s comfort meets our racing thoughts.
Action: Close your eyes, name one overwhelming thought, and say, "Lord, comfort me."

4) 1 Peter 5:7 (KJV)
"Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you."
Reflection: Casting means actively giving God your anxieties.
Action: Physically lay down a small object (coin, pebble) as a symbol of handing the worry to God.

5) Isaiah 41:10 (KJV)
"Fear thou not; for I am with thee: be not dismayed; for I am thy God..."
Reflection: God promises presence, strength, and help.
Action: Repeat "I am with you" slowly, replacing "you" with your name.

6) John 14:27 (KJV)
"Peace I leave with you, my peace I give unto you: not as the world giveth, give I unto you."
Reflection: Jesus offers a different, steady peace.
Action: Sit quietly for a minute and breathe while repeating "My peace is with me."

7) Psalm 23:4 (KJV)
"Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for thou art with me..."
Reflection: Even in deepest dark, God walks with us.
Action: Write one fear and below it write: "God is with me."

8) Psalm 46:1 (KJV)
"God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble."
Reflection: God is an immediate place of safety.
Action: Say "Refuge" as a one-word prayer whenever anxiety rises.

9) Psalm 46:10 (KJV)
"Be still, and know that I am God..."
Reflection: Stillness opens space to know God.
Action: Close eyes for 60 seconds, focusing on one short phrase: "Be still."

10) Hebrews 13:5-6 (KJV)
"I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee... The Lord is my helper; I will not fear."
Reflection: God’s presence removes ultimate reasons to fear.
Action: Speak this promise aloud before sleep.

11) Proverbs 3:5-6 (KJV)
"Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding."
Reflection: Trust replaces anxious overthinking.
Action: When you catch yourself overthinking, say: "I will trust God."

12) Romans 8:28 (KJV)
"All things work together for good to them that love God..."
Reflection: God can bring good from hard things.
Action: Name one difficulty and look for one possible good outcome.

13) Psalm 55:22 (KJV)
"Cast thy burden upon the LORD, and he shall sustain thee..."
Reflection: God sustains, not just hears.
Action: Pray: "Lord, I cast this burden on You," and visualize it leaving you.

14) Isaiah 26:3 (KJV)
"Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace, whose mind is stayed on thee..."
Reflection: Focus on God brings steady peace.
Action: Choose a short name for God you find comforting and repeat it for 30 seconds.

15) Psalm 34:4 (KJV)
"I sought the LORD, and he heard me, and delivered me from all my fears."
Reflection: God hears prayers about fear and acts to deliver.
Action: Share one fear with God in prayer and thank Him for hearing you.

16) Deuteronomy 31:6 (KJV)
"Be strong and of a good courage, fear not, nor be afraid... for the LORD thy God, he it is that doth go with thee; he will not fail thee, nor forsake thee."
Reflection: Courage comes from God’s ongoing presence.
Action: Speak "He goes with me" before a stressful event.

17) Matthew 11:28-30 (KJV)
"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."
Reflection: Jesus invites us to exchange burdens for rest.
Action: Lay hands on your chest and ask Jesus for rest for 2 minutes.

18) Psalm 56:3 (KJV)
"What time I am afraid, I will trust in thee."
Reflection: Trust is a response you can choose in fear.
Action: When afraid, say: "I will trust in You" three times.

19) Romans 15:13 (KJV)
"Now the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace in believing..."
Reflection: Hope and peace are gifts from God as we believe.
Action: Pray for hope to fill you, and write one hope you have today.

20) Jeremiah 29:11 (KJV)
"For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, saith the LORD, thoughts of peace..."
Reflection: God’s plans include peace and a future.
Action: Meditate for a minute on the phrase "plans to give you peace."

21) Lamentations 3:22-23 (KJV)
"It is of the LORD's mercies that we are not consumed, because his compassions fail not..."
Reflection: God’s mercies are renewed every morning.
Action: Each morning, name one mercy you saw yesterday.

22) John 14:1 (KJV)
"Let not your heart be troubled: ye believe in God, believe also in me."
Reflection: Believing in Jesus brings calm to a troubled heart.
Action: Pray: "Lord Jesus, calm my troubled heart."

23) 2 Timothy 1:7 (KJV)
"For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind."
Reflection: Fear is not from God—He gives power and clarity.
Action: Declare: "God gives me a sound mind" and take one calm step forward.

24) Zephaniah 3:17 (KJV)
"The LORD thy God in the midst of thee is mighty; he will save, he will rejoice over thee with joy..."
Reflection: God delights in you and is active on your behalf.
Action: Say, "God rejoices over me," and smile into the truth.

25) Psalm 27:1 (KJV)
"The LORD is my light and my salvation; whom shall I fear?"
Reflection: With God as your light, fear loses its hold.
Action: Name one fear and speak "Whom shall I fear?" over it.

26) Psalm 121:1-2 (KJV)
"I will lift up mine eyes unto the hills, from whence cometh my help. My help cometh from the LORD..."
Reflection: Our help comes from God, not from our circumstances.
Action: Look up (literally) and whisper, "My help comes from the Lord."

27) Psalm 62:8 (KJV)
"Trust in him at all times; ye people, pour out your heart before him..."
Reflection: God welcomes all honest emotional pouring out.
Action: Pour out your heart in 2–3 honest sentences to God.

28) Psalm 16:8 (KJV)
"I have set the LORD always before me: because he is at my right hand, I shall not be moved."
Reflection: Keeping God before you brings steadiness.
Action: Place a small visual reminder (cross, verse card) where you will see it.

29) Colossians 3:15 (KJV)
"And let the peace of God rule in your hearts..."
Reflection: Let peace be your guide in decisions and reactions.
Action: Pause before reacting and ask, "What would God’s peace do here?"

30) Psalm 34:18 (KJV)
"The LORD is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart; and saveth such as be of a contrite spirit."
Reflection: God draws near to those who are hurting.
Action: Pray, "Lord, draw near to me now," and breathe in God’s presence.

30-Day Reading Plan (simple)
- Days 1–7: Verses 1–7 (focus on immediate peace & prayer)
- Days 8–14: Verses 8–14 (focus on God’s presence and trusting)
- Days 15–21: Verses 15–21 (focus on God’s promises and daily rest)
- Days 22–30: Verses 22–30 (focus on courage, hope, and practicing peace)
Each day: read the verse, read the reflection, do the action (2–5 minutes).

Practical Tips for When Anxiety Strikes
- Keep 3–5 favorite verses in your phone notes for quick access.
- Use one-line versions: pick a 5–7 word phrase from the verse to repeat during breathing.
- Pair Scripture with slow breathing: read one short verse while inhaling, repeat it while exhaling.
- Journal: write the verse and three honest sentences about how it applies to your situation.
- Share: text a verse and a brief encouragement to a friend—connection reduces anxiety.

Short Prayers You Can Use
- "Lord, help me to rest in Your peace right now."
- "Jesus, be near and calm my heart."
- "Father, I cast this worry on You—thank You for sustaining me."

Closing Encouragement
These verses are tools—God uses His Word to steady us. Return to this list whenever anxiety comes. Start with a verse, breathe, act on the short step, and invite God into the moment. Small, repeated practices help faith and peace grow.
