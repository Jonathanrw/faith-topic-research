Night Anxiety Prayer Guide
A short, practical faith-based guide to calm anxious thoughts at night with prayer, scripture, and simple actions.

INTRODUCTION
If you struggle with fear, panic, or racing thoughts at night, this guide is for you. It offers short prayers, Bible promises, simple calming steps, and reflection prompts you can use immediately. Read what you need, use the practices that help, and return to this guide whenever anxiety wakes you at night.

QUICK CALM (1–3 minutes)
1. Breathe slowly: Inhale for 4 counts — hold 1–2 counts — exhale for 6 counts. Repeat 4 times.
2. Name one thing you can feel in the room (e.g., bed beneath you). Naming grounds your mind.
3. Speak one truth out loud: “God is with me.” Pause. Breathe once more.

A 5-STEP NIGHT PRAYER RITUAL (5–10 minutes)
1. Sit up or stay in bed. Take 3 slow breaths.
2. Give God the worry: Pray one sentence — “Lord, I bring this [name it] to you.” (Philippians 4:6)
3. Read a short Scripture aloud (choose from the list below).
4. Pray a short surrender: “Father, I trust You with this. Help me rest.” (Isaiah 26:3)
5. Close with thanks: Name one small thing you are thankful for tonight.

SCRIPTURES FOR NIGHT ANXIETY (read aloud)
- Philippians 4:6–7 — “Do not be anxious about anything... and the peace of God will guard your hearts.”
- Psalm 4:8 — “In peace I will lie down and sleep, for you alone, O Lord, make me dwell in safety.”
- Isaiah 26:3 — “You keep in perfect peace those whose minds are steadfast, because they trust in you.”
- Matthew 11:28–30 — “Come to me, all who are weary... and you will find rest for your souls.”
- John 14:27 — “Peace I leave with you; my peace I give you. Do not let your hearts be troubled...”
- Psalm 23:1–4 — The Lord is my shepherd... even though I walk through the darkest valley, I will fear no evil.

SHORT PRAYERS (ready to use)
Immediate Calm Prayer
Lord, I am scared and my mind is racing. Please be near. Quiet my thoughts. Fill me with Your peace. Amen.

Surrender Prayer
Father, I release this worry to You. I do not have to hold it alone. Help me trust You through the night. Amen.

Prayer Before Sleep
Jesus, I lay my head down in Your care. Guard my thoughts and dreams. Give me rest that restores my heart. Thank You for Your presence. Amen.

PRAYER FOR PANIC OR PANIC ATTACK (step-by-step)
1. Stop and breathe: 4 full breaths, focus on exhale.
2. Name the fear: “This is panic about ______.”
3. Speak a short truth: “God is with me. He has not left me.” (Hebrews 13:5)
4. Press fingers gently to a part of your body (ankle, forearm) to ground yourself.
5. Pray: “Lord, help me now. Calm my body and thoughts.” Wait and breathe until you feel steadier.
6. If needed, call a trusted friend or a crisis line. You don’t have to do this alone.

DECLARATIONS (repeat slowly)
- “The Lord is my shepherd; I lack nothing.”
- “God’s peace guards my heart and mind.”
- “I give this fear to God; He cares for me.” (1 Peter 5:7)

ACTIONABLE NIGHTLY HABIT (15–20 minutes)
1. Turn off bright screens 30–60 minutes before bed.
2. Write down your worries and a next action for each (even small).
3. Read one short Scripture aloud.
4. Follow the 5-step night prayer ritual above.
5. Choose one calming activity: soft music, warm drink, light reading.
6. Pray briefly and bless your night.

JOURNAL REFLECTION PROMPTS (use nightly or weekly)
- What worried me most tonight? ________________________________________
- What did I give to God before bed? ____________________________________
- What small sign of God’s care did I notice today? _______________________
- What one step can I take tomorrow to reduce worry? ____________________

PROMPTED SCRIPTURE READING PLAN (for the week)
Day 1: Psalm 4 & Psalm 23 (evening)
Day 2: Philippians 4:4–9 (reflect on thanksgiving)
Day 3: Isaiah 26 (practice trusting)
Day 4: Matthew 11:28–30 (rest in Jesus)
Day 5: John 14:27 & Hebrews 13:5 (remember God’s presence)
Day 6: Psalm 91 or Psalm 46 (safety and refuge)
Day 7: Review favorite verses, write a personal prayer

WHEN ANXIETY KEEPS RETURNING (a simple plan)
1. Notice pattern: time, trigger, thoughts — write it down.
2. Choose one practical change (reduce caffeine, set a worry time at 5pm, shorten news exposure).
3. Increase help: talk to a pastor, therapist, or support group.
4. Keep repeating short prayers and scriptures nightly—even when you don’t feel immediate relief. Peace is often a steady, small practice.

A SHORT NIGHTTIME SCRIPT TO MEMORIZE
“Lord Jesus, I bring my fear to You. You hold me. Fill me with peace. Help me rest in You. Amen.”

ADDITIONAL RESOURCES
- Talk with a pastor, counselor, or Christian therapist if night anxiety is frequent or disabling.
- If panic includes thoughts of harming yourself, contact emergency services or a crisis line and seek immediate help.

FINAL ENCOURAGEMENT
You are not alone in this. God sees your nights and hears your prayers. Use these simple steps and scriptures as practical tools to bring God’s peace into your evening. Come back to this guide whenever you need a steady, calm way to take your worries to God.

If you’d like, copy the prayers and declarations into your phone or a small card to keep by your bed. Keep practicing—small habits build lasting calm.
