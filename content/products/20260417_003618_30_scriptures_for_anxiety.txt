30 SCRIPTURES FOR ANXIETY

A scripture pack for Christians who need comfort, peace, and quick help when anxiety hits.
Promise: A short, scripture-based resource you can open anytime for calm, truth, and practical next steps.

HOW TO USE THIS PACK
- Keep this file on your phone or print it.
- When anxiety arrives, pick one short verse to read, breathe with, and repeat.
- Use the quick action at the end of each entry (30 simple steps) to move from feeling overwhelmed to trusting God.

QUICK THREE-STEP PRACTICE (Use in the moment)
1) Pause and breathe: 4 counts in, hold 1, 6 counts out. Repeat 3 times.
2) Read one scripture below slowly. Let a short phrase sink in.
3) Do the "Try this" step that follows the verse (speak it out loud, write it down, pray it).

SECTIONS
- Hold on to God’s peace (1–6)
- God’s presence and help (7–14)
- Truths to speak over fear (15–22)
- Promises to remember in hard places (23–30)

HOLD ON TO GOD’S PEACE

1) Philippians 4:6–7 (KJV)
“Be careful for nothing; but in every thing by prayer and supplication with thanksgiving let your requests be made known unto God. And the peace of God, which passeth all understanding, shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus.”
In plain language: Don’t be anxious — pray about everything with thankfulness and God’s peace will guard your heart and mind.
Reflection: Prayer plus thanksgiving flips worry into peace.
Try this: Say aloud: “Lord, I bring this to You with thanks.” Pause and notice one calm thought.

2) Psalm 34:4 (KJV)
“I sought the LORD, and he heard me, and delivered me from all my fears.”
In plain language: God hears you and can free you from fear.
Reflection: You are not talking into empty space — God hears.
Try this: Tell God one specific fear out loud: “I’m afraid of ___.” Then breathe and thank Him for hearing.

3) Psalm 94:19 (KJV)
“In the multitude of my thoughts within me thy comforts delight my soul.”
In plain language: When worry floods your mind, God’s comfort brings joy to your soul.
Reflection: Comfort is real and available even in mental noise.
Try this: Name one comfort from God today (friend, Scripture, prayer) and thank Him for it.

4) Isaiah 41:10 (KJV)
“Fear thou not; for I am with thee: be not dismayed; for I am thy God: I will strengthen thee... I will uphold thee.”
In plain language: Don’t be afraid—God is with you, will strengthen and hold you.
Reflection: Presence and strength are God’s promises, not wishes.
Try this: Put your hand over your heart and say, “You are with me, Lord.”

5) Matthew 6:25–26 (KJV)
“Therefore I say unto you, Take no thought for your life… Behold the fowls of the air… your heavenly Father feedeth them.”
In plain language: Worrying about basic needs won’t add life; God cares for you.
Reflection: God’s care for creation shows His care for you.
Try this: List three small things God has provided this week.

6) Matthew 11:28–30 (KJV)
“Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest… for my yoke is easy, and my burden is light.”
In plain language: Bring your burdens to Jesus; He offers rest.
Reflection: Rest is part of following Jesus, not something you earn.
Try this: Close your eyes and say, “Jesus, I bring this burden to You.”

GOD’S PRESENCE AND HELP

7) 1 Peter 5:7 (KJV)
“Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you.”
In plain language: Give God your worries because He cares about you.
Reflection: Care-casting is a spiritual habit, not a one-time event.
Try this: Write your biggest worry on paper and hand it to God in prayer.

8) John 14:27 (KJV)
“Peace I leave with you, my peace I give unto you… Let not your heart be troubled, neither let it be afraid.”
In plain language: Jesus gives a peace that lasts — don’t let your heart be troubled.
Reflection: His peace is different from the world’s; it steadies you.
Try this: Repeat: “Jesus, give me Your peace now.”

9) Psalm 23:1–4 (KJV)
“The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want… Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil: for thou art with me…”
In plain language: God shepherds you and walks with you through dark valleys.
Reflection: You are not abandoned in hard places.
Try this: Picture God leading you step by step, and breathe with that image.

10) Proverbs 3:5–6 (KJV)
“Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding…”
In plain language: Trust God fully and let Him guide you.
Reflection: Anxiety often comes from relying only on our own thinking.
Try this: Say, “Lord, I choose trust over my understanding today.”

11) Psalm 46:10 (KJV)
“Be still, and know that I am God.”
In plain language: Stop striving and remember God is God.
Reflection: Stillness helps you remember who controls the outcome.
Try this: Sit quietly for one minute and focus on God’s name.

12) Romans 8:28 (KJV)
“And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God…”
In plain language: God can use difficulties for good for those who love Him.
Reflection: This doesn’t remove pain, but it gives hope about purpose.
Try this: Name one small way God might be working good through this difficulty.

13) Isaiah 26:3 (KJV)
“Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace, whose mind is stayed on thee: because he trusteth in thee.”
In plain language: Keeping your mind fixed on God brings calm.
Reflection: Focus anchors peace.
Try this: For two minutes, repeat one short phrase like “God is with me.”

14) Psalm 55:22 (KJV)
“Cast thy burden upon the LORD, and he shall sustain thee: he shall never suffer the righteous to be moved.”
In plain language: Give God your burdens; He will support you.
Reflection: Sustained support means you don’t have to carry everything alone.
Try this: Pray, “I cast this on You, Lord.” Then do one small step forward.

TRUTHS TO SPEAK OVER FEAR

15) Hebrews 13:5–6 (KJV)
“Let your conversation be without covetousness… for he hath said, I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee. So that we may boldly say, The Lord is my helper… I will not fear.”
In plain language: God promised He won’t leave you; say boldly that He helps you.
Reflection: Remind yourself of God’s presence when fear rises.
Try this: Say: “The Lord is my helper; I will not fear.”

16) Joshua 1:9 (KJV)
“Have not I commanded thee? Be strong and of a good courage; be not afraid… for the LORD thy God is with thee whithersoever thou goest.”
In plain language: Be strong and courageous because God is with you everywhere.
Reflection: Courage grows from remembering God’s promise.
Try this: Stand up, breathe, and declare: “God is with me.”

17) Deuteronomy 31:6 (KJV)
“Be strong and of a good courage, fear not, nor be afraid… for the LORD… will not fail thee, nor forsake thee.”
In plain language: Don’t be afraid — God won’t fail or abandon you.
Reflection: God’s faithfulness is your steady ground.
Try this: Write “He will not fail me” and keep it visible today.

18) Lamentations 3:22–24 (KJV)
“It is of the LORD's mercies that we are not consumed… The LORD is my portion, saith my soul; therefore will I hope in him.”
In plain language: God’s mercy gives us hope every morning.
Reflection: Hope is renewed daily because of God’s mercy.
Try this: Start your day with: “Lord, my hope is in You.”

19) Psalm 56:3–4 (KJV)
“What time I am afraid, I will trust in thee. In God I will praise his word, in God I have put my trust…”
In plain language: When afraid, choose trust in God and praise His truth.
Reflection: Praise and trust redirect fear into faith.
Try this: Sing or speak a short praise line: “I trust in You, Lord.”

20) Romans 15:13 (KJV)
“Now the God of hope fill you with all joy and peace in believing...”
In plain language: God fills you with hope, joy, and peace as you believe.
Reflection: Belief opens the door to God’s internal work.
Try this: Pray for a fresh filling of hope right now.

21) 2 Timothy 1:7 (KJV)
“For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind.”
In plain language: Fear is not from God; He gives power, love, and self-control.
Reflection: You can choose God’s Spirit instead of fear.
Try this: Speak: “Spirit of power, love, and a sound mind, fill me.”

22) Hebrews 4:16 (KJV)
“Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.”
In plain language: Come confidently to God for mercy and help when you need it.
Reflection: Bold prayer brings real help in anxious moments.
Try this: Pray short and bold: “Help me now, Lord. Give mercy and grace.”

PROMISES TO REMEMBER IN HARD PLACES

23) Zephaniah 3:17 (KJV)
“The LORD thy God in the midst of thee is mighty; he will save, he will rejoice over thee with joy…”
In plain language: God is present, powerful, and delighted in you.
Reflection: God’s joy over you counters shame and fear.
Try this: Whisper: “God rejoices over me.”

24) Psalm 27:1 (KJV)
“The LORD is my light and my salvation; whom shall I fear?”
In plain language: With the Lord as your light and rescue, whom can you fear?
Reflection: Fear loses power when God is your light and salvation.
Try this: Picture a light in a dark room and say, “You are my light.”

25) John 16:33 (KJV)
“These things I have spoken unto you, that in me ye might have peace. In the world ye shall have tribulation: but be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.”
In plain language: There will be trouble, but Jesus gives peace and has overcome.
Reflection: Peace comes from Christ, not from a trouble-free life.
Try this: Say: “Jesus, I rest in Your victory.”

26) Psalm 139:23–24 (KJV)
“Search me, O God, and know my heart… and lead me in the way everlasting.”
In plain language: Ask God to search your heart and lead you.
Reflection: Honest prayer invites God to change anxious patterns.
Try this: Pray: “Search my heart, remove what shouldn’t be here.”

27) Nehemiah 8:10 (KJV)
“…for the joy of the LORD is your strength.”
In plain language: God’s joy is a real source of strength.
Reflection: Joy from the Lord fuels perseverance.
Try this: Recall a small joyful memory and thank God for it.

28) 1 John 4:18 (KJV)
“There is no fear in love; but perfect love casteth out fear…”
In plain language: God’s perfect love removes fear.
Reflection: The closer we draw to God’s love, the less fear controls us.
Try this: Pray: “Help me believe Your love for me.”

29) Isaiah 43:2 (KJV)
“When thou passest through the waters, I will be with thee… when thou walkest through the fire, thou shalt not be burned…”
In plain language: God promises presence and protection in life’s toughest moments.
Reflection: Presence is the promise, not a promise of easy paths.
Try this: Say: “You are with me in this, Lord.”

30) Colossians 3:15 (KJV)
“And let the peace of God rule in your hearts; to the which also ye are called in one body; and be ye thankful.”
In plain language: Allow God’s peace to govern your heart, and give thanks.
Reflection: Peace rules when you let it and pair it with thankfulness.
Try this: Make a short gratitude list (3 items) and let peace rule.

BRIEF DAILY PRACTICE (3–5 minutes)
- Morning: Read one verse, speak the short prayer, write one line: “Today I trust God with…”
- Midday: Use the 3-step practice (breathe, read, act) when pressure rises.
- Night: Read a calming verse before sleep and name one thing God did today.

SHORT PRAYERS YOU CAN USE
- “Lord, I’m anxious. Please give me Your peace and clear my mind.”
- “Jesus, I bring this worry to You. I choose to trust You.”
- “Holy Spirit, fill me with courage, love, and a sound mind now.”

FINAL ENCOURAGEMENT
You’re not alone in this. God’s Word is full of short reminders you can carry in your mind and heart. Use one verse, one breath, one small action at a time. Return to this pack as often as you need.
