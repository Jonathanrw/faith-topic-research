Panic wave? Three breaths, one verse. If your heart is pounding and your thoughts are racing, stay with me. We’re going to breathe through Psalm 46 together, calm your body, and steady your mind in God’s presence.

First, a gentle reminder: panic feels terrifying, but it is not the end of you. Your body is sounding a false alarm. This is support and prayer, not a substitute for medical care. If you have chest pain, fainting, or new concerning symptoms, seek help. For most panic episodes, you’re safe, and this wave will pass. God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble. You’re not alone.

Here’s our first aid. We’ll do box breathing synced to Psalm 46:10—“Be still, and know that I am God.” We’ll do three slow cycles together. Breathe in through your nose if you can, out through your mouth, and let the words guide your pace.

Cycle one. Inhale on “Be still.” One, two, three, four. Hold on “and know.” One, two, three, four. Exhale on “that I am.” One, two, three, four. Hold on “God.” One, two, three, four.

Cycle two. Inhale, “Be still.” One, two, three, four. Hold, “and know.” One, two, three, four. Exhale, “that I am.” One, two, three, four. Hold, “God.” One, two, three, four.

Cycle three. Inhale, “Be still.” One, two, three, four. Hold, “and know.” One, two, three, four. Exhale, “that I am.” One, two, three, four. Hold, “God.” One, two, three, four.

Let your shoulders drop. Unclench your jaw. Keep breathing gently. The verse is your rhythm. The Lord is your anchor.

Now let’s ground your senses and come back into this moment with God. Look around. Name five things you can see. The light on the wall, the shape of a window, a color that stands out, anything stable in your view. Say them softly, or think them. God is here, right where you are.

Notice four things you can feel. The chair supporting you. Your feet on the floor. The fabric against your skin. The air on your face. Press your feet down. You are held.

Listen for three sounds you can hear. Maybe a fan, a car outside, your own breath. Each sound is a reminder: you are in a real place, in real time, and God is with you in real life.

Breathe in and find two things you can smell, even if faint—the hint of soap, coffee, the air itself. Then one thing you can taste, even if it’s just the taste of breath. You are present. The storm is loud, but you are not the storm. “Be still, and know that I am God.”

Here’s what’s happening inside your body. Panic is your brain’s alarm system, flooding you with adrenaline to prepare you to fight, flee, or freeze. Your heart races, your chest tightens, your stomach flips, your hands tingle. Fast breathing blows off too much carbon dioxide, which makes dizziness and numbness worse. It feels dangerous, but in most cases it isn’t. Your body is doing what it was designed to do under threat, even when there’s no real danger. The same God who designed it can help you regulate it. Slow, steady breathing brings carbon dioxide back into balance. Naming what you see, feel, and hear tells your brain, “We are safe.” As your breath evens out, your thinking mind comes back online. The peak of panic usually rises and falls within minutes. Waves crest, then they fade. You can ride this one with God.

Let’s anchor again in Psalm 46. Verse one says, “God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble.” Not distant help. Present help. He does not mock your fear; He meets you in it. Verse ten says, “Be still, and know that I am God.” Be still doesn’t mean do nothing. It means loosen your grip, soften your body, let the truth be bigger than the feeling. You don’t have to fix this moment; you can be held through it. Take one more gentle breath with the verse. Inhale, “Be still.” One, two, three, four. Hold, “and know.” One, two, three, four. Exhale, “that I am.” One, two, three, four. Hold, “God.” One, two, three, four.

If your mind starts to spin again, speak truth to it. I am experiencing a panic wave. My body is safe. Feelings rise and fall. God is with me. I can breathe. I can wait. I can watch this pass. Isaiah 41:10 says, “Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God.” Philippians 4 says the peace of God guards our hearts and minds in Christ Jesus. Peace is not pretending everything is fine. Peace is a Person with you in the fire.

Let me pray for you. Father, You see this precious person. You know every breath, every thought, every surge of fear. Speak to their nervous system, their hormones, their muscles—peace, be still. Remind their mind that You are God and You are here. Lift the weight off their chest. Slow their breath. Quiet the alarms. Wrap them in Your refuge. Give them the wisdom to reach out for help when they need it and the courage to ride each wave with You. Thank You that Your love is stronger than adrenaline and Your presence is nearer than panic. In Jesus’ name, amen.

I want to give you a safety plan you can remember and keep. Panic wave? Three breaths, one verse. Psalm 46:10 is your anchor: Be still, and know that I am God. Breathe in four, hold four, out four, hold four—three times. Ground your senses by noticing five things you see, four you feel, three you hear, two you smell, one you can taste. Remind yourself: This is a false alarm. My body is safe. This will pass. God is with me. Reach out to someone you trust and tell them you’re having a wave. If you’re in the United States and feel unsafe or overwhelmed, call or text 988 for support; in other countries, contact your local crisis line or emergency number. If you have new or severe symptoms, seek medical care. You are not a burden. You are beloved.

Before we close, let this settle deep: you don’t have to conquer panic to be loved by God. His love meets you exactly where you are. You can return to this practice anytime—box breathing with Psalm 46, grounding your senses, and letting truth steady you. Over time, your brain learns that these waves are survivable, and your soul learns again what it already knows: God is your refuge and strength.
