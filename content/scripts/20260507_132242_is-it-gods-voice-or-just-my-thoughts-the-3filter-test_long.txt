Is that God—or just me? Use this 3-filter test. If you’ve been stuck in decision fatigue, circling the same thoughts until you feel numb, today can be the moment you breathe again.

God isn’t playing hide and seek with your heart. Jesus said, “My sheep hear my voice.” The question isn’t whether He speaks; it’s how we learn to recognize Him. When anxiety is loud and pressure is constant, discernment can feel impossible. But with a simple, repeatable rhythm, you can quiet the noise, test what you’re sensing, and move forward with confidence and peace.

Here’s the 3-filter test I use and teach: Scripture alignment, the Spirit’s fruit and peace, and wise counsel and confirmation. Then I’ll show you a gentle 48-hour rhythm to listen and test, and a short Lectio Divina practice to train your ear to God’s tone.

First, Scripture alignment. Second Timothy 3:16 says all Scripture is breathed out by God and useful to teach, correct, and train us. That means God’s voice will never contradict God’s Word or God’s character. If what you’re sensing pushes you to compromise integrity, to cling to bitterness, to diminish other people made in His image, or to elevate self above obedience, it isn’t Him. God’s convictions are specific and liberating; the enemy’s accusations are vague and suffocating. Conviction says, “This action is wrong; here’s the path back.” Condemnation says, “You are wrong, and there’s no way home.” The Shepherd leads you out of darkness with clarity and hope. Ask, does this thought mirror Jesus—full of grace and truth? Would this counsel make sense if I read it alongside the Sermon on the Mount or the fruit of the Spirit? If not, release it. If yes, hold it with open hands and move to the next filter.

Second, the Spirit’s fruit and peace versus pressure. Galatians 5 says the Spirit produces love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control. Colossians 3:15 says to let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts—like an umpire making the call. God’s voice may bring urgency, but never panic. He can be firm without being frantic. James 3:17 adds that wisdom from above is pure, peaceable, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy, and sincere. So ask your heart, what grows in me when I sit with this for a few minutes? Do I feel a grounded, courageous peace even if the decision is hard? Or do I feel frantic, shamed, and backed into a corner? Peace doesn’t mean the absence of cost. It means a settled clarity that God is with you in the cost. If the “guidance” you sense rushes you with fear, manipulates you with doom, or bulldozes you with shame, that’s not the Shepherd’s accent. His voice may stretch you, but it won’t suffocate you.

Third, wise counsel and confirmation. Proverbs 11:14 says there is safety in a multitude of counselors. God often confirms His leading through mature believers, Scripture showing up repeatedly, and doors that align with your God-given design. Share what you’re sensing with someone who knows the Bible, knows you, and isn’t afraid to lovingly challenge you. Invite them to ask questions you might be avoiding. Pay attention to consistent themes: do you keep hearing the same verse, the same nudge, the same encouragement from independent sources? Confirmation is not crowd-pleasing; it’s coherence. It’s the difference between echo-chamber agreement and Spirit-shaped agreement that points to Jesus, not your ego.

Now, the 48-hour listen-and-test rhythm. After you apply the three filters, give it forty-eight hours whenever possible. In that window, keep your heart open and your pace unhurried. Each day, take ten quiet minutes. Pray something like, “Lord, if this is You, deepen Your peace and confirm Your word. If it is not, scatter my desire and gently close the door.” Then watch what rises. Do you wake with increasing clarity and courage? Do you see providential nudges—a timely verse, unexpected counsel, a door that opens with integrity intact? Or do you feel mounting pressure and chaos? The forty-eight hours create space for God’s steadying voice to surface and for anxious noise to tire itself out. This simple pause rescues you from impulsive leaps and from endless paralysis. If a hard deadline is truly immovable, compress the rhythm—but don’t skip the posture. Even a whispered, five-minute surrender can change what you hear.

To train your ear daily, try a brief Lectio Divina. Pick a short verse, like John 10:27: “My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me.” Sit quietly and ask for the Spirit’s help. Read the verse slowly. Notice a word or phrase that seems to shimmer—maybe “hear,” maybe “know,” maybe “follow.” Read it again, and talk to God about that word. If “know” stands out, you might pray, “Jesus, I want to live known by You today. What would that change?” Sit for a minute in stillness. Let the phrase travel with you through the day. When a decision pops up, return to that word. This practice doesn’t force God to speak; it forms your attention so you can recognize when He does. Over time, you’ll learn His cadence—firm but gentle, holy yet tender, always drawing you to trust.

Maybe you’re thinking, “But what if I still get it wrong?” Remember who your Guide is. Psalm 32 says God will instruct and teach you in the way you should go; He will counsel you with His eye upon you. Romans 8:1 says there is no condemnation for those in Christ Jesus. A good Shepherd doesn’t abandon a sheep that wanders two steps off the path; He redirects. You don’t have to fear missing God like He’s a fast-moving train you might not catch. He is a Father who walks at the pace of love. The goal of discernment isn’t perfect choices; it’s deeper union with Him in every choice.

Let’s bring it together. Filter one asks, does this align with Scripture and the character of Jesus? Filter two asks, does this cultivate the Spirit’s fruit and a ruling peace rather than frantic pressure? Filter three asks, does wise counsel confirm this in a way that points to Jesus and not to my pride? Then give it forty-eight hours of listening and testing. Journal what you notice. Pray for confirmation or redirection. Practice Lectio Divina to attune your heart to God’s tone. And when you move, move with humility. If the light you have today is enough for the next faithful step, take it. God steers moving ships.
