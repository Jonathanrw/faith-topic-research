Is that God, your thoughts, or just fear talking? When you’re standing at a crossroads and your heart is loud, confusion can feel like its own kind of storm. Today, I want to give you three simple checks that calm the noise and help you discern God’s voice with confidence.

We all feel inner promptings. A sudden nudge to reach out to someone. A thought that keeps circling about a decision. A feeling that says slow down, or move now. Those impulses can come from several places. The Holy Spirit really does lead God’s people. Your own mind and emotions also generate strong ideas, dreams, and worries. Fear and past wounds can shout just as loudly, trying to keep you safe but sometimes keeping you stuck. And yes, the enemy whispers lies and accusations that distort God’s heart. The good news is that Scripture doesn’t leave us guessing. Jesus said, My sheep hear my voice. And He taught us to test what we hear. You don’t have to be afraid of missing God; the Shepherd is better at leading than you are at wandering.

Here are the three checks I use and teach. They’re simple, they’re biblical, and they work like filters that turn confusion into clarity.

First, check for Scripture alignment. God’s voice will never contradict God’s Word. All Scripture is God-breathed and useful for teaching, correcting, and training us. So when a prompting comes, hold it up to the light of the Bible. Ask, does this align with the character of Jesus and the clear teachings of Scripture? God will not nudge you to lie, to manipulate, to indulge a secret sin, or to trample another person made in His image. He also won’t push you toward self-hatred or despair, because there is no condemnation for those in Christ Jesus. Sometimes the Bible doesn’t name your exact situation, but it always reveals God’s heart. Look for themes. Is this prompting saturated with love, truth, humility, and holiness? Does it reflect the wisdom from above that James describes as pure, peace-loving, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy and good fruit? If a thought requires you to twist a verse out of context to justify it, set it down. If it draws you toward Christlikeness, keep listening.

Second, check for Christlike fruit. God’s leading may challenge you, but it won’t poison you. The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control. Ask, if I follow this prompting, what fruit will it likely produce in me and in others? Sometimes your body will still feel anxious because risk is scary, but beneath the nerves there will be a steady, anchored peace that Philippians calls the peace of God guarding your heart and mind. There’s a difference between the discomfort of obedience and the torment of confusion. Conviction from the Spirit is specific and hopeful. It points to Jesus and shows a next step. Condemnation is vague and crushing. It says, you’re a failure, and there’s no way out. One comes with an invitation; the other comes with a shove. God’s voice may be firm, but it won’t be cruel. It may be urgent, but it won’t be frantic. When you listen, look for the fruit it nourishes.

Third, check with wise counsel and the peace that follows prayer. Scripture urges us to seek counsel because plans succeed with many advisers. Bring the prompting to trusted, Scripture-shaped voices in your life. Ask them to push back, to ask questions, to pray with you. Then bring it to God, honestly. Tell Him your desires and your fears. Ask Him to close doors you should not walk through and to open the ones that are from Him. Colossians says, let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, like an umpire. After praying, do you sense a settled green light, even if your hands are trembling? Or a red flag, a check in your spirit that won’t go away? Community and prayer don’t replace obedience, but they protect it. And when counsel, Scripture, and a growing peace line up, you can move forward without second-guessing every step.

Let’s see how these checks play out in real life.

Imagine you receive a job offer with more pay and prestige, but the role will demand dishonest reporting and a schedule that quietly erodes your time with family and church. Part of you says, this is the breakthrough you’ve been praying for. Another part feels heavy. Run the checks. Does it align with Scripture? God values integrity, truth in the inward being, honest scales, and caring for what He’s entrusted to you, including your family and your soul. This prompting to compromise fails the first check. What about fruit? Following that path likely grows anxiety, pride, and secrecy, not love, joy, or self-control. You talk with a mentor who reminds you that promotion without peace is not from God and prays with you for a better door. After prayer, the glimmer fades, and a surprising calm returns, even though you don’t have a backup plan yet. You can now say no with confidence, not from fear, but from faith. God’s voice guarded you.

Here’s another. You’re washing dishes when a quiet nudge lands: text that friend you haven’t heard from in weeks and tell them they are not alone. You feel a little silly. Maybe it’s nothing. Run the checks. Does it align with Scripture? Yes. Encourage one another daily. Bear one another’s burdens. What fruit might this produce? Love, comfort, gentleness, maybe even joy if it meets a real need. You pray a simple prayer and sense a warm clarity. So you send a short message with a verse. Minutes later, they reply, I just asked God for a sign that He sees me. Thank you. That wasn’t pressure, that was partnership. That’s what it’s like to hear and follow.

If you’re thinking, this all sounds good, but I still feel foggy, remember this: growing in discernment is like growing a muscle. You learn the tone of God’s voice over time by soaking in His Word, walking in community, and taking small obedient steps. Don’t despise small impressions when they align with Scripture and bear good fruit. Also, don’t rush when big decisions require patience. God is patient with you. He’s not playing hide-and-seek with His will.

If it helps, pray this with me right now. Father, I quiet my heart before You. You are my Shepherd, and I am Your child. I surrender my need to control the outcome. Search me and know me. Expose any fear, pride, or distraction that drowns out Your voice. I submit this decision to Your Word. If it contradicts Scripture or Your character, close the door. If it leads me closer to Jesus and grows Your fruit in me, give me courage to obey. Surround me with wise counsel and let the peace of Christ rule in my heart. Speak, Lord; Your servant is listening. In Jesus’ name, amen.

One more encouragement. If you belong to Jesus, you are not on the outside trying to get God’s attention. You are loved, indwelled by His Spirit, and invited to walk with Him. Even when you misread a moment, grace covers you. He knows how to redirect. He’s not fragile. Neither is His plan for your life.
