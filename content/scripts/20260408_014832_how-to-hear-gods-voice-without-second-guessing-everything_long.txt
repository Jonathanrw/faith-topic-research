If you’ve ever prayed, “God, speak to me,” and then immediately wondered, “Was that You or just my thoughts?” this is for you. Three simple checks can help you know it’s God’s leading, not just anxious self-talk, and they can turn second-guessing into steady confidence.

Let’s name the struggle. You want to follow God. You ask for direction. Then your mind floods with what-ifs. What if I miss it? What if I make a wrong choice? What if I’m just making this up? That inner debate can keep you stuck in neutral, even when your heart longs to move. The good news is, Jesus said, My sheep hear My voice. Hearing God is part of your inheritance as His child. And God is not the author of confusion, but of peace. So how do we tune our ears to His tone?

Here’s a simple discernment grid you can use anytime. Think of it as three doors your impression must pass through: Scripture alignment, Christlike fruit, and peace with wise counsel. Scripture, Fruit, Peace plus People. If it passes all three, you can walk forward with confidence.

First, Scripture alignment. God will not contradict His Word. His voice carries the character of His Word because He never changes. Ask, does this impression line up with what the Bible says about God’s nature, truth, love, holiness, and wisdom? If the thought pushes you to deceive, to harm, to inflate pride, or to bypass integrity, it’s not Him. If it calls you to humility, repentance, courage, kindness, and obedience, you’re in the right lane. Psalm 119 says His Word is a lamp to our feet. That means you don’t need a floodlight for the next five years; you just need enough light to take the next faithful step. If you’re unsure, open Scripture and look for God’s heart on the matter. Often, the verse that brings humble clarity will rise above the noise.

Second, Christlike fruit. The Spirit’s voice bears the Spirit’s fruit: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control. Check the emotional and behavioral fruit the impression produces in you. God’s conviction is specific and invitational. It says, “Turn from this and come close,” and it leads to hope. The enemy’s condemnation is vague and crushing. It says, “You’re a failure,” and it leads to despair. One moves you toward Jesus and people; the other isolates you in shame. Even when God calls you to do hard things, His leading produces courage laced with peace, not panic laced with dread.

Third, peace plus wise counsel. Colossians says, let the peace of Christ rule—act like an umpire—in your heart. Pair that internal witness with external wisdom. Bring your sensing to a mature, trusted believer who knows Scripture and your story. Proverbs teaches there is safety in an abundance of counselors. If your inner sense of peace resonates with godly counsel, that’s a strong confirmation. If your sense of urgency collapses under the light of community, that’s a red flag. God’s voice welcomes testing. He is not threatened by your careful discernment.

Let’s contrast God’s voice and anxious self-talk because they feel different. Anxious thoughts are often loud, hurried, and absolute. They use words like always and never, push you to act now or else, and leave you more tangled the longer you sit with them. God’s voice is firm but unhurried. It makes room for questions. It carries clarity, even when the path requires faith. Anxiety catastrophizes the future; God’s voice calls you to faithful obedience today. Anxiety says, “If you don’t control this, everything will fall apart.” God says, “Be still and know that I am God.” Anxiety keeps score; God extends grace. There is therefore now no condemnation for those in Christ Jesus. When you notice condemnation, declare what’s true and let that lie go.

If you still feel torn, try a timing test. The slow-down test asks, does this hold up after 24 to 48 hours of prayer and Scripture, or does it deflate when the adrenaline fades? The sunlight test asks, does this make more sense in the morning after rest, or only at midnight when worries shout? The obedience test asks, what happens when I take one small, low-risk step in this direction? God’s guidance often grows clearer in motion. If the step produces greater peace and alignment with Scripture, keep going. If it breeds chaos or compromise, course-correct.

To quiet the mental noise, use simple journaling prompts that bring your thoughts into the light. Start by writing, Father, I sense You might be saying… Then ask, which Scriptures support this? What Christlike fruit would this produce in me and in others? What fear is making this feel urgent? If I were not afraid, what faithful step would I take today? Who are two wise believers I can share this with for feedback? End with, Lord, I surrender the outcome; show me the next right step. Writing slows the swirl and creates space for the Spirit to speak.

If you need structure, here is a seven-day listening plan you can revisit anytime. Day one is quiet. Ten minutes of stillness with a simple prayer, Speak, Lord, Your servant is listening, followed by reading Psalm 23 slowly. Let the words shepherd you. Day two is Word. Read John 10 about the Good Shepherd. Underline phrases that stand out and ask why. Day three is surrender. Hold your decision or question open-handed before God and release your preferred outcome. Pray with Jesus, Not my will, but Yours. Day four is confession. Ask the Spirit to search your heart. Confess any sin, resentment, or pride that could be clogging your hearing, and receive grace. Day five is ask. Pray James 1:5, asking for wisdom, then sit in silence and write any impressions that surface, testing them with the three checks. Day six is community. Share what you’re sensing with a trusted mentor or friend and invite honest feedback and Scripture input. Day seven is review and rest. Look back over the week. Where did you sense peace? What Scriptures repeated? What counsel resonated? Thank God for leading you, and rest in His timing.

Remember, God’s guidance is often a lamp, not a spotlight. He leads you step by step. When you practice Scripture, Fruit, and Peace plus People, you create a pathway your heart can trust. Over time, you’ll notice second-guessing loosening its grip. You’ll recognize His tone the way you recognize a loved one’s voice in a crowded room.

If you are walking through anxiety or intrusive thoughts, know that seeking pastoral care or a wise counselor can be part of God’s provision. The Spirit works through Scripture, prayer, and community. He also works through the slow, faithful practices that shape your attention. As you quiet the hurry, feed on the Word, and invite others into your process, you’ll find what Isaiah promised: you will hear a voice behind you saying, this is the way; walk in it.

So today, take one step. Ask for wisdom. Test what you hear with Scripture, Fruit, and Peace plus People. Write it down. Share it with a trusted believer. Then take the next right step with a settled heart. The Shepherd is not trying to trick you. He’s guiding you into life.
